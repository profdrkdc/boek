# Chapter 2: The Shared Ground of Ethics

What is the basis of our actions? Where do we find a foundation for ethics solid enough to withstand the storms of existence? For centuries, the answer seemed only to sow division. People found it in God, in karma, in the laws of nature, or in the cold logic of survival. Each camp defended its own foundation and rejected that of the other.

This chapter proposes a different idea: all these foundations are, without knowing it of one another, built on the same, unshakeable ground. All the great systems of meaning in human history, however different their language may be, converge toward one single, universal, and inescapable principle: the highest moral duty is the care for the future.

It does not matter which path you follow. The destination is the same.

Let us walk the **theistic path**. If you believe in a creating God who designed the universe and life, then the assignment is clear. The world is His creation, and humanity is the steward. Each generation receives creation on loan and bears the sacred duty to protect it, improve it, and pass it on. Working toward a better future — a world with less suffering, more justice, and more flourishing — is then the ultimate form of worship. It is honouring God's work by continuing it. The belief in an 'afterlife' becomes here a literal instruction: if we live well, our successors — those who live after our death — will step into a world that is once again a step closer to 'paradise on earth'.

Let us follow the **spiritual path** of reincarnation and karma. This path holds that our essence — our soul — travels a long journey through many lives. Our actions in this life determine the circumstances of the next. What is this but a beautiful, personal metaphor for the reality of genetic and cultural inheritance? Our 'soul' is the information in our DNA and the values we embody. When we act well — when we gather knowledge, build prosperity, and create a stable society — that essence 'reincarnates' into a next generation that begins with better cards. Caring for the future is the most effective way to care for your 'next life', because that next life is that of our children and grandchildren.

Finally, let us take the **scientific-materialist path**, which assumes an indifferent universe without a built-in purpose. Even here, the cold, hard logic leads to exactly the same conclusion. Evolutionary theory and systems theory show us an iron law: every system that undermines its own future destroys itself. A species that exhausts its environment goes extinct. A culture that rewards short-sightedness collapses. From this perspective, ethics is not a choice but the ultimate survival strategy. Care for the future is not a moral option but an existential necessity, dictated by the fundamental rules of existence itself.

The circle is complete. The theist calls it God's will. The Buddhist calls it the path to enlightenment. The scientist calls it an evolutionarily stable strategy. The names differ, but the assignment is identical.

This is no contradiction. This is a confirmation. Ethics is universal. There is a solid, shared ground for everyone, believer or not. Belief in God need not be replaced; it is confirmed by the logic of reality. Atheism need not search for a moral anchor; it finds one in the fundamental laws of that same reality.

And this universal ethics has an unavoidable consequence. A group that embraces this truth and consciously acts upon it — that invests in knowledge, cooperation, and the next generation — will naturally become more competent. It will come to understand the mechanisms of the world better. It will become more effective at solving problems. In the end, those who build a better future will also be those who learn how to overcome everything and everyone that threatens the wellbeing of that future. Protecting the future then becomes not only a moral duty, but also an achievable reality.
