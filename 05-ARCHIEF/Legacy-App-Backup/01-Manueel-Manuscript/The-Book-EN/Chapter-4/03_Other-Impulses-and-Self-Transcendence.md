## 3. Other Irrational Impulses and the Necessity of Self-Transcendence

Although the sexual drive is the most dominant, it is only one voice in the chorus of primal instincts that continually attempts to drown out our rational thinking. Two other powerful echoes from our evolutionary past deserve particular attention: tribalism and short-term thinking.

**Tribalism** — the deep-rooted tendency to divide the world into 'us' versus 'them' — was once a crucial survival strategy. It forged strong bonds within the group and created the necessary wariness toward outsiders who could pose a threat. In the complex, globalised world of today, however, this instinct is a recipe for disastrous conflicts. It fuels nationalism, political polarisation, and racism. It causes us to block cooperation with those we see as 'the other', even when we face collective, global problems such as climate change or pandemics. Tribalism is the enemy of universal ethics; it sabotages the scaling up that is necessary for humanity to survive as a whole.

**Short-term thinking** is an equally stubborn legacy. For our ancestors, the immediate future — the next meal, the next danger — was all that mattered. The distant future was an abstraction they could not afford. This instinct translates today into an almost pathological preference for immediate reward over sustainable gain. Companies sacrifice long-term stability for quarterly figures; politicians choose popular measures that saddle future generations with the bill; and as individuals we give in to immediate gratification at the expense of our future health and prosperity. This instinct is the direct antithesis of our core task: it is the systematic sacrifice of the future for the present.

This animal heritage — the all-consuming sexual drive, blind tribalism, short-sighted thinking — forms a relentless assault on our competence. It is the gravity that pulls us toward the easiest, most primitive solution, away from the difficult, rational path upward.

Here lies the deepest assignment of the human being: **self-transcendence**. This is not a vague spiritual term, but a concrete, functional necessity. It is the conscious and merciless process of using our neocortex — our rational, thinking brain — to recognise, understand, and where necessary overrule our older, instinctive impulses. It is the ultimate act of willpower and intelligence: the human being rewriting its own programming.

Overcoming our animal nature is not a denial of who we are. It is the fulfilment of our potential. It is the recognition that the instruments that helped us survive in the past have become the obstacles to our future. The next step in human evolution is not biological, but conscious. It is the step from an animal that can think, to a being that *chooses* to think — even when every fibre of its body screams to act on instinct. This is the true birth of the competent human being.
