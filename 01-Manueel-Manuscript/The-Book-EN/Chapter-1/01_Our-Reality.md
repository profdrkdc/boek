# Chapter 1: Our Reality

The world we live in is not perfect, not finished, not complete. It is an unfinished construction site. There is much work to do, and today more than ever we are confronted with the fact that we must take our responsibility in it. It is we who can and must take charge, we are the ones who must give direction to the future.

The stories we used to tell ourselves — about a predetermined fate or a mysterious director behind the scenes — were training wheels and handles with which a young humanity tried to stay upright while feeling powerless against all it did not understand and could not control. But full comfort, and for everyone, these stories never completely offered; there was too much injustice and senseless evil in the world. For centuries we sought salvation from the outside, but our responsibility lies within ourselves. It is this responsibility that is the only source of meaning in life, and that can provide the only comfort you need in life.

The universe is a giant mechanism that runs according to its own laws. It is not against us; it is simply the raw material. Nature is a rough construction waiting for optimization. Disease is a biochemical puzzle that we can solve. An earthquake is a geological fact that we can build structures against. Where nature stops being favorable to us, our task as designers begins. Humans have the unique gift of adding compassion and efficiency to a system that naturally does not possess them.

The natural basis is only the foundation; we are also the architects of our society. By learning from our mistakes in the past, mistakes we made through ignorance and fear, we must give direction to the future. Out of responsibility towards those who come after us, who are in fact largely dependent on us for their well-being and happiness, for what kind of world they are given. Our history was, and is, full of struggle, misery and woe... but there has been an unmistakable line of progress, not always linear, not always equally fast, but over the long term there has always been progress.

This book is not a search for the 'why' of the past. That question distracts us. We look at the 'how' of the future. This book is an invitation to intellectual emancipation on the scale of the entire civilization, it is an invitation to action. The world is a system with bugs, and we are the debuggers, the programmers. The architects and the builders. Suffering is the output of an outdated system; well-being must be the output of the new system.

Welcome to the workshop. The real work can begin.
